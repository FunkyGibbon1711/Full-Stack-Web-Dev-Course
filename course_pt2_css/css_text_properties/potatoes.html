The potato is a starchy food, a tuber of the plant Solanum tuberosum and is a root vegetable native to the Americas.The plant is a perennial in the nightshade family Solanaceae.

Wild potato species can be found from the southern United States to southern Chile. The potato was originally believed to have been domesticated by Native Americans independently in multiple locations, but later genetic studies traced a single origin, in the area of present-day southern Peru and extreme northwestern Bolivia. In the Andes region of South America, where the species is indigenous, some close relatives of the potato are cultivated.
